Mì Quảng

Description

Mì Quảng, meaning 'Quảng Noodles' in English, is a hearty and flavorful noodle dish that originated from Quảng Nam Province in Central Vietnam, which is where I came from. It is a staple dish in my region and is, by far, my favorite dish ever. This recipe uses pork and prawns as toppings, but there are other variations that use different ingredients. The recipe was translated into English from this site.

Ingredients

  • Pork tail bones: 500g
  • White onions: 50g
  • White radish: 50g
  • Prawns: 500g
  • Pork belly: 500g
  • Wild onion bulbs: 100g
  • Peanuts: 100g
  • Shallots: 50g
  • Turmeric: 20g
  • Quail eggs: 20
  • Cashew oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Peanut oil: 1 cup
  • Mì Quảng noodles
  • Toasted sesame rice crackers
  • Garnishes: mustard sprouts, lemon basil, spearmint, shredded banana blossoms, one unripe plantain, white cabbage.

Instructions

  1. Mise en place

    • Boil a pot of water, blanch the pork tail bones for about 5 minutes, then drain, rinse and let dry.

    • Peel the white onions, leave them whole. Peel and cut the white radish into long sections.

    • Rinse the wild onion bulbs, then pour them into a vegetable-washing basket and rub to remove the outer layer, then cut off the black tips, let dry and crush.

    • Finely chop the shallots.

    • Take about 20g of peanuts, wash them, let them dry, and crush them finely. Roast the remaining peanuts thoroughly.

    • Thoroughly grind the turmeric.

    • Thoroughly wash the garnishes. Shred the cabbage. Peel the plantain, slice it thinly, and soak in water with a bit of lemon juice to prevent browning. Mix all the garnishes together.

    • Boil the quail eggs.

    • Blanch the pork belly in boiling water with ½ teaspoon of salt for about 2 minutes, then rinse, let dry, and slice into thin pieces about 3cm wide.

    • Remove the heads, veins, and legs of the prawns. Rinse them with 1 teaspoon of salt and water twice, then let dry.

    • Heat a pan with peanut oil until it steams, then turn off the heat and let it cool for about 5 minutes.

  2. Simmer the broth

    Put the blanched bones into a pot with 3 liters of water, bring to a boil, and simmer for about 1 hour. Add the whole onions and white radish, skim off any foam. Simmer for another 30 minutes, then turn off the heat.

  3. Marinate the pork belly

    Marinate the pork belly for 30 minutes with 1 teaspoon of salt, 1 teaspoon of MSG, 1 teaspoon of pepper, 2 teaspoons of fish sauce, 2 teaspoons of chopped purple onions, 1 teaspoon of crushed shallots, and 1 teaspoon of sugar. Mix well to ensure the pork belly absorbs all the seasonings.

  4. Marinate the prawns

    Marinate the prawns for 30 minutes with 1 teaspoon of salt, 1 teaspoon of MSG, 1 teaspoon of pepper, 1 teaspoon of chopped shallots, and 1 teaspoon of crushed wild onion bulbs. Mix well

  5. Sauté the wild onion bulbs

    Heat a pan with ½ of the pre-heated peanut oil, add ½ of the wild onion bulbs, and stir until golden, crispy, and fragrant. Drain and set aside the fried wild onion bulbs and the oil.

  6. Sauté the prawns

    Heat the pan used for the wild onion bulbs, add the prawns and stir-fry until the prawns turn red, then turn off the heat.

  7. Flavor the broth and assemble the toppings

    Heat a pan with about 3 tablespoons of peanut oil, add the remaining crushed wild onion bulbs, and stir until fragrant. Add 20g of crushed peanuts and 1 tablespoon of turmeric, stir well, then turn up the heat and add the pork. Cook until just done, then add 1.5 liters of broth, the quail eggs, and season with 1 tablespoon of salt, 1 tablespoon of MSG, 2 tablespoons of sugar, and 4 tablespoons of fish sauce. Add the sautéed prawns and 2 tablespoons of cashew oil for color. Bring to a boil, skim off any foam, and turn off the heat.

  8. Serve and enjoy

    Separate the Mì Quảng noodle strands, blanch in boiling water, and place in a bowl. Toss the noodles with a bit of the oil used for the wild onion bulbs. Arrange the pork, prawns, quail eggs, pour over the broth, and sprinkle with chopped scallions, fried wild onion bulbs, and roasted peanuts. Mix well and serve with the garnishes, toasted rice crackers, and Hội An chili sauce on the side.
